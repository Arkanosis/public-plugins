Assembly

This is the first release of the draft assembly of the Western lowland gorilla Gorilla gorilla gorilla. The DNA sample came from a 30-year-old female, Kamilah, owned by the San Diego Wild Animal Park, and sequencing and assembly is provided by the Welcome Trust Sanger Institute.

Sequencing was undertaken using two separate methods, the results of which were combined to create the first full de novo gorilla assembly. Firstly, traditional capillary sequencing was used to provide a 2X assembly and assembled using the Phusion assembler. Secondly the genome was sequenced using the newer Illumina machines, which provide a high depth of coverage but in much shorter reads. The Illumina reads were then mapped onto the assembly, enabling the identification and removal of sequencing errors and contamination; a wide range of viral and other contaminants were identified, leading to a cleaner, more accurate draft assembly.

The total number of capillary reads used in the assembly after clean-up is 7901471; the N50 size for contigs is 2094 bp and the N50 size for supercontigs is 11277 bp.

Annotation

Owing to the fragmentary nature of this assembly, it was necessary to arrange some scaffolds into "gene-scaffold" super-structures, in order to present complete genes. There are 9673 such gene-scaffolds, with identifiers of the form "GeneScaffold_1". The gorGor1 assembly contains 1366111 contigs and 609860 scaffolds.

The gene-building procedure on assembly version gorGor1 identified 16724 protein coding genes and 472 pseudogenes as well as miRNA genes, misc_RNA genes, and rRNA, snoRNA and snRNA genes.
