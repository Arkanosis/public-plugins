Annotation

The current gene set for anole was built using a modified version of the standard Ensembl genebuild pipeline. The species-specific sequence resources (cDNAs and proteins) for anole are very limited, hence the majority of the gene models are based on genewise alignments of proteins from other species, mainly mammals and birds.

We used the top three levels of protein existence criteria in Uniprot to filter out all predicted and uncertain proteins. Chicken proteins annotated by Ensembl were also aligned to the genome. To improve the accuracy of models generated from these proteins, the genewise alignments were made to stretches of genomic sequence rather than to 'miniseqs'. Additional genes were predicted using anole EST evidence of which a subset was included in the final gene set. ESTs were also used to add UTRs to the gene models where possible.

Using annotations from the Oxford Functional Genomics group, we have now refined the existing gene models and included additional genes to the set.
