Species trees

The Compara pipelines use various species trees:

  • The protein trees pipeline follows the NCBI taxonomy.
  • The ncRNA trees pipeline uses the NCBI taxonomy as a base and flattens the three following sub-trees: Eutheria, Sauria, and Clupeocephala.
  • The CAFE pipelines (gene gain/loss trees) also follows the NCBI taxonomy, and use the TimeTree database for the branch lengths.
  • The alignment pipelines follow a binary species tree that is maintained in-house (in accordance with the literature).
  • The constrained elements and conservation scores pipelines need branch lengths, and we compute branch lengths for four sub-trees of the previous tree by running phyloFit (part of the PHAST package) on our multiple alignments. They are available in Newick format: mammals, sauropsids, amniotes, fish.
