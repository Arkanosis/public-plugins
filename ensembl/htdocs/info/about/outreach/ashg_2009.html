Ensembl Interactive Workshop

Paul Flicek and Xose Fernandez from the European Bioinformatics Institute will present an interactive workshop on Ensembl in the 59th Annual Meeting of The American Society of Human Genetics (ASHG) next Friday 23rd October at noon [Room 315, Convention Center].

Requirements: You must bring your laptop (make sure your battery is fully charged) and your computer should have a wireless card to be able to participate. For questions about the workshop, contact Xose Fernandez at xose@ebi.ac.uk.

Interactive Workshop, Friday 23 from 12.00-13.00

Ensembl provides an infrastructure for genomic information, from its storage through to analysis and visualisation. This workshop offers participants the possibility of gaining hands-on experience in the use of the Ensembl genome browser providing the necessary background information.

Ensembl uses data from ENCODE and other genome-wide functional datasets to produce and display more integrated views of genome function. These functional elements may harbour more variants than protein coding sequences opening new ways of exploring the relationship between variation and function.

By attending this interactive wireless workshop you will learn about databases, the Ensembl Genome Browser and data mining with BioMart that help scientists understand the human and 48 other animal genomes.
